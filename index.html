Sister Rosetta Tharpe

The Godmother of Rock 'N' Roll

Here is timeline of Sister Rosetta Tharpe's life:

  • 1915 - Born Rosetta Nubin in Cotton Plant, Arkansas
  • 1921 - Tharpe joined her mother as a regular performer in a traveling evangelical troupe
  • Mid 1920’s - Settled in Chicago, IL with her mother where they performed religious concerts at the Roberts Temple Church of God in Christ as well as occasionally traveling to church conventions throughout the country. Tharpe became known as a musical prodigy, standing out in an era where female guitar players was rare.
  • 1934 - Married Thomas Thorpe, a COGIC preacher. Their marriage only lasted a few years, but she adopted a version of her husbands name to create her stage name, Sister Rosetta Tharpe
  • 1938 - Left her husband and moved to New York City where she recorded for her first time - a 4 sided Decca Records release backed by Lucky Millinder’s jazz orchestra. “Rock Me”, “That’s Alright”, and “The Lonesome Road” were instant hits
  • 1943 - With the rise of nightclub performances and popularity among the secular audiences, Tharpe became shunned by some in the gospel community
  • 1944 - Her song “Strange Things Happening Every Day” was the first gospel song to appear on the Billboard magazine Harlem Hit Parage. This has been called the first rock and roll record.
  • 1946 - Tharpe meets Marie Knight. The two formed a popular touring duo as well as became lovers which shook the gospel world.
  • 1949 - Knight and Tharpe part ways. Tharpe puts on a concert at what is now Altria Theater along with the Twilight Singers whom became Tharpe’s future background singers, renaming them The Rosettes
  • 1951 - Marries her manager, Russel Morrison in a wedding that drew 25,000 paying customers which was followed by a performance at Griffith Stadium in Washington, D.C
  • 1957 - Month long UK tour
  • 1964 - Toured Europe as part of the Blue and Gospel Caravan
  • 1970 - Tharpe’s performances curtailed by a stroke
  • 1973 - Died as a result of another stroke in Philadelphia, PA
